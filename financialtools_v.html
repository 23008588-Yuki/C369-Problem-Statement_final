Student Bank Accounts

Many banks in Singapore offer special accounts for students aged 18 and above. These accounts typically come with lower fees or no fees, higher interest rates on savings, and sometimes perks like discounts or cashback.

Debit Cards

Debit cards linked to student accounts allow easy access to funds for daily expenses and online purchases, while limiting spending to available funds.

Budgeting Apps

Mobile apps like Seedly, Money Lover, or YNAB (You Need A Budget) can help students track expenses, set budgets, and manage their money effectively.

Student Credit Cards

Some banks offer credit cards designed specifically for students, with lower credit limits and features to help build credit responsibly. These cards often come with rewards or cashback on specific categories like dining or online purchases.

Educational Grants and Scholarships

Explore government grants and scholarships available for higher education or specialized training programs. These can help reduce or eliminate the need for student loans.

Financial Literacy Workshops

Attend workshops or seminars offered by schools, banks, or financial institutions to learn about budgeting, managing debt, investing basics, and building credit.

Online Banking and Mobile Apps

Familiarize yourself with online banking platforms and mobile apps provided by banks, which offer convenience in managing accounts, transferring funds, and making payments.

CPF Education Scheme

Under the Central Provident Fund (CPF) Education Scheme, eligible students can use their parents' CPF savings to pay for their tertiary education fees.

Student Loans and Financial Aid

Research options for student loans or financial aid available through institutions, government agencies like SkillsFuture Singapore, or private organizations to fund higher education or skills training.

Investment Platforms

Explore beginner-friendly investment platforms like Robo-advisors (e.g., StashAway, Syfe) or Regular Savings Plans (RSPs) offered by banks, which allow students to start investing with lower amounts and minimal knowledge.
